About Me

I am Ella Guest, a VTCT Foundation Senior Research Fellow at the Centre for Appearance Research. As a Chartered Psychologist with the British Psychological Society (BPS) and a Registered Health Psychologist with the Health and Care Professions Council (HCPC), I am dedicated to understanding and improving the psychosocial wellbeing of individuals with appearance-altering conditions. My research focuses on fostering positive adjustment and promoting positive body image.

Psychosocial Support for Appearance-Altering Conditions

I aim to provide comprehensive support for individuals dealing with the challenges of appearance-altering conditions. By developing targeted psychosocial interventions, I help improve mental health and quality of life, promoting resilience and positive self-perception.

Positive Body Image and Adjustment

My work explores how individuals can develop a healthy and positive relationship with their appearance. I use innovative methods, such as photo-elicitation interviews, to deeply understand and enhance body image perceptions, helping people thrive despite societal pressures or personal challenges.

Social Media and Appearance Diversity

I investigate how social media can be leveraged to promote acceptance of appearance diversity and reduce stigma. My goal is to create more inclusive online environments that celebrate all forms of beauty and support individuals in their journey toward self-acceptance.
